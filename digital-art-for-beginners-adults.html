Digital Art for Beginners (youth & adults)

Learn how to use professional drawing and painting software to create illustrations on the computer from start-to-finish. Whether you're interested in picture books, animation and comics, or you just want to be able to make your own graphics for your projects, having a knowledge of digital art can allow you to create more efficiently, expand your repetoire of tools, and equip you with useful skills for working with the publishing or media industries.

No prior experience with digital art is needed. A basic knowledge of computer operation, and some art fundamentals, is recommended.

This course is geared towards digital illustration - that is, drawing and painting on the computer. While many of the skills and tools I teach in this class can be transferred to graphic design, that won't be the focus of the course.

☆ A NOTE ON AGE: This class is open to youth and adults, and is designed to have a heavier focus on industry best practices and creative process than my digital art class for teens. I haven't specified a lower limit to the age cutoff, so that high schoolers or college students who want to benefit from the longer class time, and are comfortable learning alongside working adults, can enroll in the class too if they wish.

Equipment

Students must bring their own equipment for this course. Please make sure you have one of the following combinations of equipment. Equipment should be charged prior to classes, as outlet plugs are limited!

OPTION 1: (recommended)
  • Laptop • Windows 7 or later, OR Mac OS 10.10 or later
    Please make sure your laptop is reasonably up-to-date and has adequate memory to run digital graphics software.
  • Pen tablet/graphics tablet
    (examples: *Wacom Intuos, *Wacom Intuos Pro, Huion INSPIROY models)
OPTION 2:
  • Touchscreen tablet or laptop with stylus
    Stylus must have pen pressure sensitivity
    (examples: Surface Pro, *Surface Book, iPad Pro & Apple Pencil, *Wacom Cintiq Companion, Wacom MobileStudio Pro, Samsung Galaxy Tab with S Pen)

* marks equipment I have personally worked with and would recommend. I cannot vouch for other options. If you have any doubts or questions about equipment, please email me at janice@youngartistsplace.com!

Software

For beginners to digital art, I teach with FireAlpaca (free for both Windows and Mac), Paint Tool SAI (~$70 after currency conversion, Windows only), and Clip Studio Paint Pro. ($63, Windows, Mac, and iPad). I use these programs in my classes because they are low-cost and intuitive to learn for newbies.

Students are free to take their pick from any of these programs, and install it on their devices prior to the course.
